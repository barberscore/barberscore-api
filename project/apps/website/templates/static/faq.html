Frequently Asked Questions

How does it work?

First, with your permission, we connect to your photos through your cloud provider. Then, our system indentifies great family shots (usually including the kids) that would be appropriate to send to the grandparents. Finally, we text a copy of that picture to you (and anyone else you designate) each day, every day. It will bring a smile to your face every time you see it.

Wait—this sounds like magic. How do you find "great family photos"?

It's a two-step process: first, our system does an automated pre-screen to skip all the photos you wouldn't want us to see. (For instance, our facial recognition algorithms can spot children and if kids are in the shot then it's assumed to be family-friendly.) Then, of the pre-screened photos, our team conducts a personalized quality review to ensure that nothing inapporpriate is sent. (Computers are great for some things, but picking great photos is still a job for a human.) That's it!

Do I get to approve what's sent?

Yes, each account is set to enforce pre-approval by default. "Pre-Approval" means that as the account owner you'll be asked to approve each selected Daily before sending. It's easy to approve: simply reply with a simple 'Y' to send. Or, if you prefer, you can disable pre-approval which means that each daily will be sent automatically without any additional effort.

What about work photos and other irrelevant shots?

Our system identifies family photos, typically ones with the kids, and ignores everything else. Work photos, pictures of food, selfies, and other non-family shots are skipped entirely. Of those which pass this first test, we conduct an individual quality review to ensure the best possible photos are sent. (Easy test: if the photo isn't appropriate for grandma, then it is ignored.)

What if I don't have kids? Can you send other photos?

For the time being we are only set up to identify and send great family photos that include kids. In the future we may add other preferred subjects such as vacation shots, but for now we're strictly family-only.

What if I don't backup my photos in the cloud?

Forgive us for being presumptious, but you really should! Your photos are your most precious memories and can be easily lost to theft, fire, or just plain old computer failure. But more to the point, if you don't keep your photos online we can't organize them. We work in the cloud to keep our service affordable. Some backup services (like Flickr) are free and secure, and most are very easy to set up. Contact us at 415.659.9863 if you'd like us to help you get your photos backed up through a cloud service and we'll do everything we can to get you started.

How do you get access to my pictures?

You need to grant us permission to do so through your cloud provider (currently Dropbox or Flickr.) Once granted, our system is able to process your images (copies only — the originals are not touched.) It all happens automatically, including all updates, so the service remains current even as new pictures come in with no additional effort required (although you can revoke access at any time.)

Which cloud services do you support?

We currently support Dropbox and Flickr. You can also signup and request a new cloud service; we're working every day to add to our list of supported providers.

Are my pictures safe?

Yes. We don't touch the original photographs; we only send copies via text. So your pictures are 100% safe.

What's the cost?

The cost is $19 a month, which includes two recipients (yourself and one other.) We also provide a free one-month trial, and you can cancel at any time. Additional recipients are $5 per month.

What about privacy?

We don't copy, keep, or use your photos for any purpose other than this service. Also, we automatically skip non-family photos, but if you have pictures of a particularly sensitive nature then the best idea is not to keep those shots in the cloud at all.

Do I need to select photos to upload?

No, that's our job! Choosing between photos is the hardest part about getting organized; that's what we're good at. This is a "set it and forget it" service.

Where did you come up with the name "Dinadesa"?

I have two young daughters, named Sarina and Alessa, and my pet-names for them are 'Sarina-Dina' and 'Alessa-Desa'. The company was essentially inspired by them (and their grandparents' insatiable desire for photographs) so Dinadesa was born. BTW, it's pronounced "DEE-na DEH-sa".
